VennPool

VennPool is a mobile app that aims the alleviate messy communication associated with organizing large carpools. We worked with mentors and investors as a part of the startup incubator, The Basement.

View Development


RESEARCH

The following user pain points served as a reference for the design. This particular case study was done on the project manager's outback adventures group.


  • Too many steps for the one organizing the carpools
  • Who has a car, who needs a ride
  • Long lists of messages
  • Extra manual messages when someone suddenly can't make it anymore

INITIAL DESIGN

Our initial design was very "engineered." We came up with an object-oriented design that organized the user-flow into groups, events, and rides. A user would be a part of a group and within that group the user can have any number of events. Each event would contain all the possible rides people within the group can join or offer a ride. This object-oriented style was stemmed off the idea of trying to integrate a user account control system, so random users can't just join group-restricted rides.

FEEDBACK

After some User Testing and Design Critiques, we discovered that our initial design involved too many steps and was too confined within its object-oriented model. The complexity may push away new users. Users suggested to avoid or minimize account creation.


REDESIGN

As the incubator showcase night approached, we decided to focus our efforts into implementing the our first design's feedback and developing the minimum viable product. Due to the too many steps for creating an account, we removed our account creation and replaced it with a Facebook login api. Once a user logs in or creates an account through Facebook login, their Events are parsed into their feed, and from there they can see and offer rides. Logging in with Facebook solves a lot of the problems mentioned from our feedback phase.

  • Less steps to sign-in and create an account.
  • Trust issue with a new app. A user is less likely to provide information to an app that is new to the market.
  • User Account Control is internally managed by Facebook's group/event system.
